Usage

  • rencheck.ninja uses rental 'cards' to evaluate the performance of real estate property.
  • Add the input data to the 'Market' tab to see the cash outflow, income and yield the property will generate.
  • You can add cards with the add button on right side of the toolbar, .
  • If you want to manipulate the assumptions of all rental cards, click into the global defaults menu on the right side of the toolbar, .
  • You can also manipulate the assumptions on per rental basis in the 'Assumptions' tab of each rental card.
  • Check the 'Details' tab to see more information about the calculations, an abridged mortgage ammortization schedule, etc.

Background

rentcheck.ninja gives quick estimates of rental performance. The idea is to support workers and families, not institutions. By entering just a few numbers and making a few assumptions, the performance can be roughly and quickly estimated.

Tips & Tricks

    Usage tips

  1. Outflow has to be less than rent to have the green background
  2. Confirm and save defaults for your situation (income tax rate, property tax for your area, downpayment, etc.)
  3. The add button on the right side of the tool bar lets you add another rental calculator for comparisons.
  4. If you're returning to rentcheck.ninja, save the rental information with save button to save yourself the typing.

    Performance tips

  1. Leveraging the property (putting down a smaller downpayment) can boost yield and potential for capital gains, but dramatically increases risk.
  2. Finding low cost properties can boost yield as well, but that can decrease potential for capital gains and increased vacancy rates if the area does not support high quality renters.
  3. A one bedroom can earn more per buck than two bedroom. Again, there is greater risk of a higher vacancy rate.
  4. Although harder to manage, properties outside of your area might perform much better (rent relative to price is not always constant).

    Speculation

    1. Buy low, sell high!
    • Selling real estate is a lengthy process with high transaction costs. Reallocating capital is crazy painful (unless your a broker).
    • If your selling high, you're also buying high unless you can sit on cash for a number of years losing out on rental income.
    • Selling also means giving up the existing investment's tax basis, which means getting like performance is that much harder.
    • If your leveraging the investment, it also means restarting the 30 year clock for the mortgage.
    • If you are re-allocating to a different property, ask your tax advisor about 1031 exchanges to postpone taxes.
    2. Capital Gains
    • rentcheck.ninja does not speculate on the capital gains, which can contribute to a majority of returns in markets like San Francisco or New York.
    • The focues of this site is the promotion of finding passive income that rentals can generate.
    • Like wine, rentals get better with age because property taxes and interest are pegged to purchase price, but rents move with the market.
    • If you're speculating on price, during your evaluation don't forget exit costs, mostly capital gains tax, depreciation recapture and transaction costs.
    3. Macoeconomic factors are too hard to predict and introduce too much variability in modeling asset prices, so I'll leave that to the risk takers.
